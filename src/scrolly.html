Education Days, in which New York City elementary school students are invited to attend a Columbia women’s basketball game through a partnership with the New York City Department of Education, represent some of the Lions’ most attended games.

Every Education Day since 2017 has drawn a crowd of over 2,000 attendees, the majority of whom are elementary schoolers.

Leading up to the first conference home game of the 2017-18 season, all eyes were on senior Camille Zimmerman, who was poised to break the Columbia record for most career points scored.

Although the Lions lost the game, the match was a win for Zimmerman. Scoring a total of 22 points, she beat the previous record of 1,746 points—held by Leonard "Buck" Jenkins, CC ’93—by a single free throw.

The Light Blue’s Levien 2018-19 finale, with an attendance of 1,032, attracted the most fans of any non-Education Day game of the season. In addition to Senior Night, the game touted two other promotions: the Columbia-Barnard Consortium celebration and Girl Scout Day, with over 100 girl scouts in attendance.

The first game dedicated to celebrating Columbia’s LGBTQ+ community—an event that included a t-shirt giveaway for the first 500 attendees—came at the tail end of the Lions’ run to their first-ever spot in the Ivy League Tournament.

The team’s next match, the regular season finale against Penn, would be its last game until 2021 following the cancellation of the Ivy Madness game against Princeton.

The Lions’ dominant start of the season—the Penn game would be their sixth straight win and 14th win overall—was stymied only slightly by an Omicron spike at the beginning of 2021 that would result in game cancellations, postponements, and attendance restrictions.

The Jan. 26 Penn game was the first home game to admit fans after the University’s Dec. 29 spectator ban. Columbia’s limited attendance policy at this game meant that only 105 spectators were able to watch in person.

The following regular season home games, all Ivy play with promotional events and giveaways, would garner the highest average attendance of any four-game stretch in the 2017–2022 period.

Although the Light Blue ultimately lost to Seton Hall, this game capped off the winningest season in Columbia women’s basketball history. This game also marked the deepest run in the WNIT by any Ivy League team—a record that would be broken the next year when Columbia advanced all the way to the WNIT finals.

This game against Princeton, the third in a four-game-long home run (all of which had promotional events and giveaways), drew the highest attendance of any game in the team’s history, packing a crowd of 2,653 fans into the seats of Levien.

The Lions lost to Princeton, breaking their five-game winning streak and allowing Princeton and Harvard to catch up with them and tie for first place in the Ivy rankings.

The Light Blue’s first-ever conference championship win cemented its position as either the number one or two seed at the Ivy League Tournament (tied with Princeton).

The team would go on to compete in the WNIT, making it to the championship game before being defeated by the University of Kansas.
